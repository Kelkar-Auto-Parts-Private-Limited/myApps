1. VMS Overview

After login, the VMS sidebar shows navigation items based on your assigned role(s). The top bar displays the current page title, your location, and a database connection indicator. A sync timestamp shows the last data refresh.

The VMS tracks vehicle trips end-to-end: booking, gate entry/exit, material receipt, and approval. Each step is handled by a specific role. The Dashboard provides an operations overview with date filters (1D, 1W, 1M, 1Y) and tabs for Overview, Trip Details, and Reports.

2. Trip Booking Role

Viewing Your Trips

The Trip Booking page shows a sticky header with date-range buttons (1D, 1W, 1M, 1Y), a "Book New Trip" button, and a search bar. Below is a list of your booked trips with status indicators.
Step 1: Navigate to Trip Booking in the sidebar.
Step 2: Use date range filters or search by Trip ID / Vehicle number to find trips.

Creating a New Trip

Step 1: Click + Book New Trip. A modal opens displaying the auto-generated Trip ID.
Step 2: Select the trip type. A trip type badge appears showing your selection.
Step 3: Choose the origin and destination locations from the dropdowns. Select the vehicle type and specific vehicle.
Step 4: For multi-segment trips, add additional segments specifying each leg's from/to locations.
Step 5: Review the trip details and click Save. The trip status becomes "Booked".
The trip rate is auto-calculated from the Trip Rate Master based on vehicle type and route. If no rate exists, the admin will need to add one.

3. KAP Security Role

The KAP Security page has three tab buttons at the top: EXIT, ENTRY, and SPOT. Each tab shows a count badge of pending items. Below the tabs are trip segment cards showing vehicle details.

Gate Exit (Outgoing Vehicles)

Step 1: Click the EXIT tab. You see booked trips awaiting gate exit.
Step 2: Tap a trip card to open the Gate Exit modal. Review vehicle number, driver, and trip details.
Step 3: Capture the challan photo using the camera button. Verify the vehicle condition.
Step 4: Click Confirm Exit to record the gate-out timestamp.

Gate Entry (Returning Vehicles)

Step 1: Click the ENTRY tab. You see trips where vehicles are returning.
Step 2: Tap a trip card, capture the entry challan photo, and perform a vehicle check.
Step 3: Click Confirm Entry to record the gate-in timestamp.
Photos are saved and viewable later via the photo lightbox (click any photo thumbnail to enlarge, save, or share).

4. Material Receiver Role

The Material Receipt page shows a search bar at the top and a list of pending deliveries below, followed by a "My Material Receipts" history section with date filters.
Step 1: Navigate to Material Receipt in the sidebar.
Step 2: Review the pending deliveries. Each card shows Trip ID, vehicle, and material details.
Step 3: Tap a pending delivery to confirm receipt. Verify the material matches the trip details.
Step 4: Confirm to record the material as received. The trip advances to the next stage.
Use the search bar to quickly find a trip by ID or vehicle number among pending items.

5. Trip Approver Role

The Trip Approvals page shows a search bar, pending approval cards, and a "My Approvals" history table with date filters and a running total amount.
Step 1: Navigate to Trip Approvals in the sidebar.
Step 2: Review pending trips. Each card shows the trip route, vehicle, cost, and all prior completion steps.
Step 3: Click Approve to confirm the trip, or Reject with a reason if there is an issue.
Step 4: Approved trips appear in the "My Approvals" history with the total approved amount displayed.
Date filters let you review historical approvals by day, week, month, or year.

6. Plant Head Role

The Plant Head has visibility over the Dashboard and can perform approvals for their location.

Step 1: Open the Dashboard. The Overview tab shows statistics cards for trips, vehicles, and costs at your location.
Step 2: Use the Trip Details tab for a granular trip-by-trip breakdown.
Step 3: Use the Reports tab for summarized data and export options.
The Plant Head is assigned per location in the Location Master. Contact your Admin to update this assignment.

7. Spot Vehicle Entry

On the KAP Security page, the SPOT tab allows recording ad-hoc vehicles that arrive without a pre-booked trip.
Step 1: Click the SPOT tab on the KAP Security page.
Step 2: Click Record Spot Vehicle Entry. A popup titled "Spot Vehicle Entry" appears.
Step 3: Enter the vehicle number, driver details, and purpose of visit.
Step 4: Save to create an ad-hoc entry record. This can later be converted to a formal trip if needed.
