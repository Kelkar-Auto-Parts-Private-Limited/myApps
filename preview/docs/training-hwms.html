HWMS Training Guide

Role: HWMS User

This guide covers the Hardware/Warehouse Management System for managing containers, invoices, inventory, material requests, and payments for international shipments.

Table of Contents
1. HWMS Overview 2. Dashboard 3. Main Invoices (MI) 4. Containers 5. Sub-Invoices (SI) 6. Material Requests 7. Parts Master 8. Payments & Outstanding 9. Other Masters

1. HWMS Overview

The HWMS sidebar is divided into two sections. OPERATIONS: Dashboard, Main Invoices (MI), Containers, Inventory, Sub-Invoices (SI), Material Requests, Payments, Outstanding. MASTERS: Customer Master, Part Master, Carrier Master, Ports, Other Masters, Company Details. Each item shows a count badge. A sync timestamp appears in the top bar.

The HWMS tracks goods from international suppliers through containers, invoicing, warehousing, and dispatch to customers. The top bar shows your name and a database status dot.

2. Dashboard

Step 1: Click Dashboard in the sidebar. The dashboard loads with summary statistics and key metrics.
The dashboard displays cards with aggregate data: total invoices, containers in transit, pending material requests, outstanding payments, and inventory levels.
The dashboard auto-refreshes. Check the sync timestamp in the top bar to see when data was last updated.

3. Main Invoices (MI)

Viewing Invoices

The Invoices page has two tabs: "By Sea" and "By Air", each with a count. Below are column headers with inline search/filter fields: MI No., Date (with date range shortcuts CY/LY/M/All), Container, Pickup Date, MI Status (Draft/RFD/Dispatched/Warehouse), SI status, Payment status, Reach Date, Amount ($), Gross Weight, SI Amount, Received, Balance, Tariff %, and Tariff ($). Header totals show aggregate amounts.
Step 1: Click Main Invoices in the sidebar. The Sea tab is shown by default.
Step 2: Filter by MI number, date range, or status using the inline controls. Click column headers to sort.
Step 3: Use the date shortcuts (CY = Current Year, LY = Last Year, M = Month, All) for quick filtering.

Creating a New Invoice

Step 1: Click + Add MI. A full-page edit form opens.
The form is divided into color-coded sections: Invoice Details (blue) with FY, MI No., and Date; Container & Shipping (purple) with container number and transport mode (By Sea / By Air); Customer (green) with Buyer, Consignee, Ports, Payment Terms, and Delivery Terms; Line Items (amber) with a pallet-based add row for parts, quantities, and rates.
Step 2: Fill in the FY (auto-set), MI suffix number, and date.
Step 3: Select or link a container. Choose transport mode (Sea/Air).
Step 4: Select the buyer (customer) and consignee. Set ports and terms.
Step 5: Add line items: select part number, enter quantity. Pallet numbers auto-increment. Rates pull from the Part Master.
Step 6: Click Save Invoice. Use Reset to clear all fields.

Bulk Operations

Use checkboxes to select multiple invoices for bulk delete or bulk download of MI data. The Export button downloads all visible invoices as Excel. Import allows uploading invoice data from .xlsx files.

4. Containers

Step 1: Navigate to Containers in the sidebar. The page shows all containers with their current status.
Step 2: Add a new container with its number, carrier, and shipping details.
Step 3: Track status transitions as containers move through the lifecycle (e.g., Booked, In Transit, At Port, Warehouse, Delivered).
Containers are linked to Main Invoices. When editing an MI, you can select or create a container association.

5. Sub-Invoices (SI)

Step 1: Navigate to Sub-Invoices in the sidebar.
Step 2: Sub-invoices are derived from main invoices and sent to individual customers. Create an SI by selecting the parent MI and specifying which line items to include.
Step 3: Track SI amounts against MI amounts. The MI list shows the SI total and balance columns.

6. Material Requests

The Material Requests page shows a list of requests with status indicators and count badges.
Step 1: Navigate to Material Requests in the sidebar.
Step 2: Click + Add to create a new request specifying required parts and quantities.
Step 3: Track request lifecycle: Created, Dispatched, Closed.
Material requests pull from the Inventory and Part Master. Ensure parts are registered before creating requests.

7. Parts Master

The Parts Master page shows a searchable table with columns: Photo, Part No., Rev, Description, UOM, Net Weight (kg), HSN, Packing, Ex-Works ($), Final Rate ($), Status, Actions.
Step 1: Navigate to Part Master under Masters in the sidebar.
Step 2: Search by part number or description. Click + Add Part to create new entries.
Step 3: Use Export/Import for bulk management of part data via Excel.
Parts have two rate fields: Ex-Works (base price) and Final Rate (including freight). Air shipments can use either rate based on the "Air Freight Cost" setting on the MI.

8. Payments & Outstanding

Step 1: Navigate to Payments to record and track payment receipts against invoices.
Step 2: Navigate to Outstanding for a summary view of unpaid balances by customer.
Step 3: Use import matching to reconcile bank payment records with invoice data.
The MI table shows Received ($) and Balance ($) columns with running totals in the header, giving you a quick view of payment status across all invoices.

9. Other Masters

Under the MASTERS section in the sidebar:

  • Customer Master — manage buyers and consignees
  • Carrier Master — shipping carriers for containers
  • Ports — ports of loading and discharge
  • Other Masters — additional reference data (payment terms, delivery terms, etc.)
  • Company Details — your company information for invoice headers
Keep masters up to date as they populate dropdown options throughout the HWMS.
