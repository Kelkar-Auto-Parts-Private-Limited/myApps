HRMS Training Guide

Role: HRMS User

This guide covers the Human Resource Management System for managing employees, attendance, salary, and organizational masters.

Table of Contents
1. HRMS Overview 2. Employee Management 3. Attendance & Salary 4. Attendance Rules 5. Salary Calculation 6. Masters 7. Print & Export

1. HRMS Overview

The HRMS has a left sidebar with: Dashboard, Employees, Attendance & Salary, Attendance Rules, Masters (expandable: Plant, Category, Employment Type, Team, Department, Sub Department, Designation), and a Refresh Data button. A connection widget shows DB status at the bottom.

The top bar shows a hamburger menu (mobile), current page title, and your username. Navigate between sections using the sidebar.

2. Employee Management

Viewing Employees

The Employees page shows filter pills at the top: All, On Roll, Contract, Piece Rate, Visitor. Below is a table with columns: #, Emp Code, Name, DOJ, Plant, Type, Team, Category, Status, Actions. A second header row provides per-column search/filter inputs.
Step 1: Navigate to Employees in the sidebar.
Step 2: Use the type filter pills to narrow by employment type. Use column search fields for specific lookups.
Step 3: Click column headers (Emp Code, Name, DOJ, etc.) to sort ascending/descending.

Adding an Employee

Step 1: Click + Add Employee. A full-page form opens.
Step 2: Fill in: Employee Code, Name, Date of Joining, Plant, Employment Type, Team, Category, Department, Sub Department, Designation, and status.
Step 3: Save the record. The employee appears in the list.

Importing Employees

Step 1: Click Import and select an Excel/CSV file. The system reads employee data in bulk.
Step 2: Use Bulk Update to modify existing records via spreadsheet.
Step 3: Use Import DOJ/DOB to update date fields specifically.
Always Export first as a backup before performing bulk imports or updates.

Change Requests

Click the Change Requests tab next to the Employees tab. Pending change requests show a count badge. Review and approve/reject modifications submitted by other users.

3. Attendance & Salary

The Attendance & Salary page shows a monthly grid. Each row is an employee; each column is a day of the month. Cells display attendance status codes (P, A, P/2, H, EL) with color coding. Entry/exit times and Present On Time (POT) hours are tracked per cell.
Step 1: Navigate to Attendance & Salary in the sidebar.
Step 2: Select the month and year. The grid loads all employees with their daily attendance.
Step 3: Click any cell to view or edit entry/exit times. The system auto-calculates present status based on hours inside.

4. Attendance Rules

The system applies these rules automatically:

Hours InsideStatusP Count
8 hrs 15 min or moreP (Present)1
6 hrs 30 min to 8 hrs 15 min (Staff, max 2/mo)EL (Early Leave)1
4 hrs to 8 hrs 15 minP/2 (Half Day)0.5
Less than 4 hrsA (Absent)0
Holiday (H) and Paid Holiday (P) days are non-working. Any hours worked on these days count as OT @S (special overtime) for non-Staff employees. Staff employees do not receive overtime.
The Attendance Rules page in the sidebar provides a complete reference with OT formulas and time rounding rules.

5. Salary Calculation

On-roll salary is calculated from attendance data:

  • Total Present (P) days drive the basic salary proration
  • OT hours (working day) and OT @S hours (holiday) are computed per the rules
  • Statutory deductions (PF, ESI, PT) are applied based on configured settings
  • The salary settings section lets you configure per-employee or per-category rates
PL (Privilege Leave) management is accessible to track leave balances and encashment.

6. Masters

Expand the Masters section in the sidebar to manage organizational data:

  • Plant — company/plant locations for employee assignment
  • Category — employee categories (Staff, Worker, etc.)
  • Employment Type — On Roll, Contract, Piece Rate, Visitor
  • Team — team groupings within the organization
  • Department / Sub Department — organizational hierarchy
  • Designation — job titles and levels
Each master page has an Add button and a table with edit/delete actions. Keep these up to date as they drive dropdowns across the HRMS.

7. Print & Export

Export: Click the Export button on the Employees page to download the full list as Excel.
Print formats: Attendance and salary sheets support print-friendly layouts. Use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P) for clean output.
Bulk print: Salary slips and attendance summaries can be generated for multiple employees at once.
